WELCOME TO KERALA!

Flanked by the Arabian Sea in the West, the towering Western Ghats in the East and networked by 44 interconnected rivers, Kerala is blessed with a unique set of geographical features that have made it one of the most sought after tourist destinations in Asia. A long shoreline with serene beaches, tranquil stretches of emerald backwaters, lush hill stations and exotic wildlife, are just a few of the wonders that await you once you crossover to the other side. And what's more, each of these charming destinations is only a two hour drive from the other - a singular advantage no other place on the planet can offer. Kerala prides itself for being the flag bearer for not just how a culture can respect its past but also march forward with growth & progress as well. Hundred percent literacy, world-class health care systems, India's lowest infant mortality and highest life expectancy rates are among a few of the milestones that the people of the state are extremely proud of

Rich Folk Music and Dance

Kerala boasts a vibrant array of folk dances and musical forms, deeply rooted in its culture and traditions. These include classical forms like Kathakali and Mohiniyattam, as well as numerous folk dances such as Theyyam, Thiruvathirakali, and Oppana, each with unique characteristics and performances. Kerala's music scene is equally rich, with Sopana Sangeetham, a temple-based musical style, and Mappila Pattu, a Muslim folk music tradition, being prominent examples.

Authentic Kerala Cuisine

Kerala's cuisine is a vibrant mix of vegetarian and non-vegetarian dishes, characterized by the use of rice, coconut, and an array of spices. Signature dishes include appam with stew, puttu and kadala curry, and various seafood preparations like meen curry and karimeen pollichathu. The cuisine also features festive meals like the traditional Kerala Sadhya, a grand vegetarian feast served on a banana leaf.

Kerala Festival

Kerala, often called "God's own country," boasts a vibrant array of festivals throughout the year, reflecting its rich cultural and religious diversity. Some of the most prominent festivals include Onam, Vishu, Thrissur Pooram, and various boat races like the Nehru Trophy Boat Race and Aranmula Snake Boat Race. These festivals are characterized by traditional rituals, music, dance, and communal harmony.
