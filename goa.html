WELCOME TO GOA!

According to Ancient Indian Mythology is said that Goa was reclaimed from the sea. It is believed that Sixth Avatar of Vishnu Sage, Parshuram created the Sahyadri range and struck an arrow into the western seas. The arrow is said to have sent the seas rolling back to create Gomantak or Goa.Goa was under Portuguese rule for about 450 years. Afonso de Albuqureque, first portuguese attacked Goa and occupied it. Due to Portuguese rule over Goa, here Christian religion spread very fast. In Goa Hindu, Muslim, Christian religions are found. Here temples, churches and mosques are existed in many numbers. All community festivals are celebrated in this state with an enthusiasm.

Rich Folk Music and Dance

Goa's folk music and dance traditions are a vibrant blend of indigenous and western influences, reflecting the region's rich history and diverse cultural heritage. These performances are integral to religious festivals like Shigmo and Carnival, as well as joyful celebrations. Key dance forms include Dekhni, Fugdi, Dhalo, Goff, and Ghode Modni, often accompanied by traditional instruments like the ghumat, harmonium, and cymbals.

Authentic Goan Cuisine

Goan cuisine is a vibrant mix of Portuguese, Hindu Saraswat, and Muslim culinary traditions, resulting in a unique blend of flavors, spices, and cooking styles. Rice, fish, and coconut are staples, deeply rooted in the region's coastal location and tropical climate. Dishes are known for their balance of spicy, tangy, and sweet notes, often incorporating coconut milk, vinegar, and a variety of local spices

Goan Festival

Goa, known for its vibrant culture, hosts a variety of festivals throughout the year. Some of the most popular include the Goa Carnival, Shigmo, Sao Joao, and Ganesh Chaturthi. Other notable celebrations include the Feast of St. Francis Xavier, Bonderam Festival, and the Sunburn Festival.
