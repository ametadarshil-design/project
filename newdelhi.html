WELCOME TO NEW DELHI!

New Delhi is the capital of India, also serving as a district within the larger National Capital Territory of Delhi (NCT). It's the seat of the Indian government, hosting the Rashtrapati Bhavan, Sansad Bhavan, and the Supreme Court. While often used interchangeably with Delhi, New Delhi specifically refers to the planned, British-built city that became the capital in 1931.

Rich Folk Music and Dance

Delhi is a hub for both folk dance and music, showcasing a vibrant mix of classical and traditional styles. You can find various folk dance forms like Bhangra, Kathak, and Bharatanatyam, alongside music genres such as Hindustani and Carnatic. Delhi also features unique folk music forms like savela, banra geet, and suhag geet.

Authentic Delhi Cuisine

Delhi's cuisine is a vibrant mix of ancient Indian and Mughal influences, with street food playing a significant role. Must-try dishes include Butter Chicken, Nihari, Chole Bhature, and various chaats like Gol Gappe and Dahi Bhalle. The city also boasts a range of kebabs, biryanis, and parathas, particularly from Paranthe Wali Gali.

Delhi Festival

Delhi hosts a wide array of festivals throughout the year, reflecting its diverse culture and vibrant atmosphere. Some of the most prominent festivals include Diwali, Holi, Durga Puja, and Republic Day. Other significant festivals celebrated in Delhi include Eid, Christmas, Lohri, and various regional festivals like Chhath and Phoolwalon ki Sair.
